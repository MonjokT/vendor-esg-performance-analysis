01 — Project Overview

What this project is about

As a Supply Chain Analyst at APEX Corporation, I was tasked with building a structured ESG (Environmental, Social & Governance) scoring framework to assess the performance, risk profile, and compliance readiness of our entire vendor base.

The business challenge was clear: APEX had no standardised way of measuring vendor sustainability, ethical conduct, or environmental responsibility across its 64 active supply chain partners. Procurement decisions were largely driven by cost and availability — with no visibility into governance risk or long-term supplier viability.

My role was to design and execute the full analytical pipeline — from data collection and cleaning, through scoring and segmentation, to visualisation and actionable recommendations for the procurement team and senior leadership.

Business Problem

No standardised ESG scoring existed for vendor selection or contract renewal. Procurement was blind to governance and environmental risk across the supply chain.

My Role

Supply Chain Analyst — responsible for the full project lifecycle: framework design, data collection, cleaning, scoring, analysis, dashboard development, and stakeholder reporting.

Outcome

A reusable ESG scoring model, a live Excel workbook, an interactive HTML dashboard, and a written report surfacing 10 high performers and flagging 8 governance-risk vendors for immediate review.

02 — Methodology

How I approached the work

The project followed a structured five-phase analytical process, from raw data consolidation to stakeholder-ready deliverables.

1
Data Collection & Consolidation
Gathered vendor information from procurement records, internal supplier registration forms, and direct questionnaires. Consolidated responses from 64 vendors across 45 distinct supply categories into a single structured dataset. Each vendor was assigned a unique ID (e.g. Vend-0001) to maintain traceability throughout the analysis.
Excel · Data Entry · Vendor Surveys
2
Data Cleaning & Standardisation
Cleaned the raw dataset to remove duplicates, standardise category names (noting inconsistencies such as "Fire Equipment" vs "Fire Equipments"), resolve missing Social scores, and validate all ESG component values fell within the 0–100 range. Flagged 2 vendors as "Pending" awaiting final submissions. Applied TRIM(), PROPER(), and IF() functions to enforce data consistency.
Excel Functions · Data Validation · TRIM · PROPER
3
ESG Scoring Framework Design
Designed a three-pillar scoring model: Environmental (carbon footprint, waste management, resource efficiency, compliance), Social (labour practices, diversity & inclusion, community impact, health & safety), and Governance (transparency, anti-corruption, compliance, ethical conduct). The Overall ESG Score was calculated as a weighted composite of these three pillars. Performance tiers were then assigned: High Performance (≥ 85), Average Performance (70–84), and Low Performance (< 70).
Scoring Model · AVERAGE · IF · Nested Logic
4
Analysis & Segmentation
Conducted multi-dimensional analysis using Pivot Tables to segment vendors by performance tier, category, assessment status, and pillar scores. Identified top performers, lowest governance scorers, and category-level ESG trends. Applied AVERAGEIF(), COUNTIF(), RANK(), and LARGE() to surface insights across the vendor population. Cross-referenced observed strengths with corrective action recommendations.
Pivot Tables · AVERAGEIF · COUNTIF · RANK
5
Visualisation & Reporting
Built a multi-sheet Excel workbook with formatted tables, conditional formatting, and embedded charts for each analytical view. Developed an interactive HTML dashboard (Power BI-style) with live filtering, bar charts, a donut chart, score distribution histogram, and full vendor ranking. Prepared a summary report with key findings, risk flags, and strategic recommendations for the procurement leadership team.
Excel · Conditional Formatting · HTML · Chart.js · Pivot Charts

Key Excel Formulas

The following formulas were central to building the scoring model, segmentation logic, and summary statistics across the workbook.

Overall ESG Score
=AVERAGE(D2, E2, F2)
Calculates the Overall ESG Score as a simple average of the three pillar scores (Environmental, Social, Governance). Applied across all 64 vendor rows.
Performance Classification
=IF(H2>=85,"High Performance", IF(H2>=70,"Average Performance","Low Performance"))
Assigns each vendor to a performance tier based on their Overall ESG Score using nested IF logic. Three tiers: High (≥85), Average (70–84), Low (<70).
Average ESG by Category
=AVERAGEIF($C$2:$C$65, A2, $H$2:$H$65)
Computes the average ESG score for each vendor category. Used in Pivot Table summaries and the Category Analysis sheet to benchmark category performance.
Count of High Performers
=COUNTIF(K2:K65,"High Performance")
Counts the number of vendors in each performance tier. Used in the Overall Performance Status sheet to produce the distribution breakdown (High / Average / Low).
Assessment Completion Status
=IF(H2="","Pending","Completed")
Auto-assigns "Completed" or "Pending" status based on whether the Overall ESG Score field has been populated. 62 completed, 2 pending.
Global Average ESG
=AVERAGEIF(J2:J65,"Completed",H2:H65)
Calculates the average ESG score only for vendors with a Completed assessment status — excluding the 2 pending vendors from the benchmark figure.
Top N Vendor Score
=LARGE($H$2:$H$65, 1)
Returns the Nth largest ESG score in the dataset. Used to identify the top 10 vendor scores and build the ranked leaderboard view. LARGE(range, 1) = highest score (90).
Vendor Rank
=RANK(H2, $H$2:$H$65, 0)
Assigns a rank to each vendor based on their Overall ESG Score (0 = descending, highest score = Rank 1). Used in the Top 10 and Full Ranking views.
Min / Max Pillar Scores
=MIN(F2:F65) / =MAX(F2:F65)
Returns the minimum and maximum values for each ESG pillar (ENV, SOCIAL, GOV) to surface the scoring range and identify outlier vendors at both extremes.
Governance Average (Completed)
=AVERAGEIF(J2:J65,"Completed",F2:F65)
Calculates the average Governance pillar score (74.6) across all completed assessments only — used in KPI summary cells and the dashboard header statistics.

"The AVERAGEIF and nested IF formulas formed the backbone of the scoring model — letting me compute category benchmarks and performance tiers dynamically as new vendor data was entered, without manual recalculation."

— Analytical Approach Note

05 — Key Findings

What I found & what it means

Six material findings emerged from the analysis, each carrying direct implications for APEX's vendor management strategy.

Finding 01

Governance is the highest-risk ESG pillar

The Governance pillar showed the widest score spread (51–90, std dev 7.5) — significantly more variable than Environmental scores (std dev 5.1). This means transparency, anti-corruption practices, and ethical conduct vary dramatically across APEX vendors, creating concentrated procurement risk.

Finding 02

High performers concentrate in mechanical & electrical categories

All 10 high-performing vendors (ESG ≥ 85) supply mechanical materials, electrical equipment, bearings, lubricants, or critical plant spares. These technically demanding categories appear to correlate with stronger operational discipline and governance structures.

Finding 03

Building & Construction is the most governance-fragile category

Building and Construction vendors recorded an average ESG of just 71 — the lowest among multi-vendor categories — and contain the single Low Performance vendor (ESG: 60). Governance scores in this group averaged below 65, well below the 74.6 overall mean.

Finding 04

96.9% assessment completion — strong data reliability

62 of 64 vendors completed their ESG assessments, giving the analysis a high-confidence data foundation. The 2 pending vendors (both in the Low Performance tier based on partial data) did not materially skew the results, but should be prioritised for completion.

Finding 05

The majority of vendors cluster in the 75–80 ESG score band

Over 60% of completed vendors scored between 75 and 80, indicating a broadly competent but largely undifferentiated middle band. This creates an opportunity to elevate average performers through targeted development programmes rather than wholesale vendor replacement.

Finding 06

Category naming inconsistencies mask true performance clusters

The raw dataset contained 45 distinct category names but many were duplicates or near-duplicates (e.g. "Fire Equipment" vs "Fire Equipments", "Mechanical Equipment" vs "Mechanical Materials"). Cleaning and standardising these would reduce the category count by ~30% and sharpen category-level benchmarking.

07 — Recommendations

Strategic actions for APEX procurement

Based on the findings, six strategic recommendations were presented to the procurement leadership team to strengthen APEX's vendor ESG posture over the next 12 months.

🔒

Prioritise Governance Corrective Action Plans

Issue formal Corrective Action Requests (CARs) to the 8 vendors with governance scores below 70. Require documented improvement plans within 30 days, with re-assessment at 90-day intervals.

High Priority
⭐

Formalise Strategic Partnerships with Top 10

Establish preferred vendor status and long-term framework agreements with the 10 high-performing vendors (ESG ≥ 85). Prioritise them in sourcing decisions and consider them for expanded scope.

High Priority
🏗

Targeted Uplift for Building & Construction

This category recorded the weakest average ESG (71) and contains the only Low Performance vendor. Conduct category-specific vendor development workshops focused on governance and environmental compliance.

High Priority
📋

Standardise Vendor Category Naming

The 45 category names contained at least 12 near-duplicate entries. Standardising to a clean master list would sharpen category benchmarking, reduce confusion in reporting, and improve data quality for future analysis cycles.

Medium Priority
🔁

Establish Annual ESG Re-Assessment Cycle

Embed this scoring model into the annual vendor management calendar. Re-assess all vendors annually, track score movement year-on-year, and use ESG performance as a formal criterion in contract renewal decisions.

Medium Priority
📊

Complete the 2 Pending Vendor Assessments

Both pending vendors have partial data suggesting Low Performance classification. Completing their assessments will close the data gap and ensure no governance risks are being under-reported in leadership dashboards.
